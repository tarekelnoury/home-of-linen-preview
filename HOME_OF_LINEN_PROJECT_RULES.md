# Home of Linen — Permanent Project Rules

These rules apply automatically to every new feature, page, component, section, category, product page, collection, landing page, campaign, and future development unless explicitly overridden.

## Core Principle

Design and develop mobile-first.

- Assume 70–75% of visitors browse on mobile.
- Assume 25–30% browse on desktop.
- Desktop visitors may have higher conversion rates and larger baskets, but mobile is the primary experience.
- Every design decision should prioritize mobile first, then scale elegantly to desktop.

## Mobile-First Rules

- Begin every UI implementation with the mobile layout.
- Treat desktop as an enhancement, not the primary version.
- Pages must load quickly on mobile.
- Interfaces must be usable with one hand.
- Products should appear with minimal scrolling.
- Tap targets must be large and comfortable.
- Avoid unnecessary clutter.
- The experience must feel fast, calm, and premium.

## Hero Banner Rules

Category hero banners are part of the Home of Linen identity.

- Use responsive image cropping.
- Preserve the product as the focal point.
- Preserve typography and tagline when they are part of the image.
- Never crop the main product.
- Never allow important text outside the visible area.
- Desktop should use wide cinematic presentation.
- Mobile should resize intelligently so customers understand the category without excessive scrolling.
- Hero images should not consume the entire first mobile screen.
- Customers should begin seeing products shortly after landing.

## Product Discovery Rules

Products are the priority. Do not hide products behind excessive content.

Collection page hierarchy:

1. Hero
2. Category title
3. Small elegant tagline
4. Filters
5. Products

## Product Card Rules

Every product card should prioritize clarity.

Always display where applicable:

- Product image
- Product name
- Price
- Available sizes
- Available colours
- Clear action button

Cards should remain visually clean with generous spacing and no visual noise.

On mobile, product cards must follow a consistent vertical order:

1. Product name and category
2. Product image
3. Colour swatches, size selectors, and variant selectors
4. Price, quantity selector, and Add to Basket button
5. Description and supporting details

Mobile product controls must never overlap, feel cramped, or sit far away from the image. Swatches should remain comfortable to tap and may scroll horizontally when there are many colour options.

## Product Image Gallery Rules

- When a product has more than one image, desktop controls should appear as subtle left/right arrows over the image.
- Desktop gallery arrows should only become visible when the customer hovers over, focuses, or interacts with the image area.
- Do not use large text buttons such as “Photo 1” or “Photo 2” on product images.
- On mobile, hide gallery arrows and allow customers to swipe smoothly between product images.
- Mobile gallery images must fit within the frame and should not crop important product details.
- Image gallery controls must feel minimal, premium, and quiet so the product remains the focus.
- Apply this rule to all future product image galleries unless explicitly overridden.

## Mobile Navigation Rules

- Navigation must be simple and thumb-friendly.
- Filters should collapse into drawers.
- Search should remain easily accessible.
- Customers should never need excessive taps to reach products.

## Product Page Rules

Product pages should build confidence.

Always include where applicable:

- Large product gallery
- Easy swipe images
- Clear size selector
- Clear colour selector
- Material information
- Care instructions
- What is included
- Delivery information

## Cross-Selling Rules

Cross-selling should feel curated, not promotional.

Recommend appropriate matching or companion items:

- Matching Pillowcases
- Matching Flat Sheets
- Matching Fitted Sheets
- Matching Duvet Covers
- Comforters
- Mattress Toppers
- Pillows
- Towels
- Home Fragrances

## Desktop Rules

Desktop users spend more time comparing products.

Desktop layouts should:

- Show more products.
- Use larger comparison spaces.
- Display additional recommendations.
- Encourage building complete bedroom collections.
- Use wider layouts without clutter.
- Prefer whitespace over crowded layouts.

## WhatsApp Rules

WhatsApp ordering is a core sales channel.

Always maintain:

- Sticky WhatsApp access on mobile.
- Easy WhatsApp access on desktop.
- Automatic product information inside WhatsApp messages where possible:
  - Product name
  - Size
  - Colour
  - Price
  - Page URL

## Performance Rules

Performance is part of the brand experience.

- Optimize images.
- Lazy-load below-the-fold content.
- Compress assets.
- Avoid heavy animations.
- Avoid unnecessary JavaScript.
- Maintain excellent mobile performance.
- Never sacrifice speed for visual effects.

## Brand Identity Rules

Every page must feel unmistakably Home of Linen:

- Minimal
- Calm
- Premium
- Warm
- Timeless
- Editorial
- Elegant
- Modern

Never use loud colours, visual clutter, or discount-marketplace styling. Luxury should be communicated through restraint.

## Design Inspiration

Align design decisions with the philosophy of:

- Zara Home
- Brooklinen
- Boll & Branch
- Premium boutique hotel websites

Use them as inspiration for spacing, typography, imagery, product presentation, and composition. Never copy directly.

## UX Philosophy

Customers should feel:

- Shopping is effortless.
- The website is calm.
- Products are premium.
- Ordering is simple.
- The experience is trustworthy.

Every interaction should reduce friction. Every design choice should improve clarity. Every feature should help customers complete purchases with confidence.
